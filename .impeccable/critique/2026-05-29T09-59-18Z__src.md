## Overall Impression

Golf Partner is more thoughtful than its surface density suggests. Feed / Play / History is the right app shape, setup is staged, and the product understands weekend tournament behavior. The next improvement is not adding visual excitement; it is protecting the live-round path and letting recap/social moments carry the personality.

## What's Working

- The bottom navigation maps well to the real jobs: Feed, Play, History.
- Setup has a sensible staged flow with player slots, course selection, tees, scoring, and review.
- Offline and recovery states are stronger than typical casual scoring apps: sync sheets, save errors, feed fallbacks, and conflict UI are present.

## Priority Issues

1. P1 - Muted text tokens fail WCAG AA and are widely reused. The product explicitly requires accessibility, but the current muted colors are too low contrast in both themes: light muted on background measured 3.16:1, light muted on card 3.50:1, dark muted on background 3.04:1. Evidence: src/theme/tokens.js:16, src/theme/tokens.js:46. Fix by raising muted/secondary contrast and auditing disabled, caption, helper, timestamp, and empty-state text.

2. P1 - Scorecard overload during play. The header exposes sync, edit, grid toggle, running view, official leaderboard, and camera, while the bottom area adds notes, hole picker, and next/finish controls. Evidence: src/screens/ScorecardScreen.js:1186, src/components/scorecard/HoleView.js:272. Make stroke entry dominant, keep next/finish primary, and move secondary actions into one More sheet.

3. P1 - Stats feels like an analytics warehouse. Six tabs, scope controls, indexes, advanced sections, and Shame cards push the History area toward admin dashboard density. Evidence: src/screens/StatsScreen.js:29, src/screens/StatsScreen.js:492, src/screens/StatsScreen.js:1811, src/screens/StatsScreen.js:2887. Default to a social recap with three to five high-signal sections, then tuck advanced analysis behind an Advanced Stats path.

4. P2 - First-time scoring setup asks too much too early. Five scoring modes plus requirements appear before the user has committed to the round format. Evidence: src/components/scoringModes.js:8, src/components/ScoringModePicker.js:44. Keep Stableford as the recommended default and hide ineligible/advanced formats behind More formats.

5. P2 - System consistency is drifting. Spanish sync/media strings appear inside an English product, and ceremonial gold is used for routine badges, fallbacks, and accents. Evidence: src/components/SyncStatusSheet.js:15, src/components/SyncStatusSheet.js:94, src/components/MemoriesRoundRow.js:27, src/theme/tokens.js:64, src/screens/FeedScreen.js:87. Pick one locale per build path and reserve gold for winners, round completion, official status, and shareable memory moments.

## Persona Red Flags

- First-timer: scoring taxonomy such as Sindicato, Best Ball, and Worst Ball may feel like tournament administration before the app has delivered value.
- On-course scorer: the scorecard competes with itself; stroke entry is surrounded by analytics, icons, sync, camera, notes, and navigation.
- Group organizer: tournament settings mix share, players, stats, scoring mode, edit, finish/reopen, delete, and round actions in one dense sheet.

## Minor Observations

- Some start tiles use larger radii and card shadows than the documented restrained system suggests; bring routine tiles closer to the 14-16px range.
- Uppercase overline labels are doing too much routine structural work. Reserve them for rare metadata, not every section divider.
- Playfair works for clubhouse moments, but should stay out of dense operational controls.
